Let's Learn About the Sun!

The sun provides the Earth with much needed energy. Without it vegetation wouldn't grow and thus animals would not have that source of nourishment. Even though the sun is only a mere piece of the larger structure known as the Solar System. What we have also discovered is that although other planets and bodies in the Solar System may not possess life, the Sun is just as influential to them.

Some Facts About the Sun:

Age 4.6 billion years
Mass (1.98855 +/- 0.00025) x 1030 kg
Volume 1.41 x 1018 km3

Things you didn't know...

  1. The Sun accounts for 99.86% of the mass in the solar system.
  2. Over one million Earth's could fit inside the Sun.
  3. One day the Sun will consume the Earth.
  4. The energy created by the Sun's core is nuclear fusion.
  5. The Sun is almost a perfect sphere.

More fun facts!

  • The Sun is classified as a yellow dwarf star.
  • The Sun will eventually be about the size of Earth.
  • It takes eight minutes for light to reach Earth from the Sun.
  • The Sun is halfway through its life.
  • Temperatures inside the Sun can reach 15 million degrees Celsius.
